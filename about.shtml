About

 

Hiya! Welcome to my online portfolio. As you might have guessed, I am Michael Rochette. Here's a brief overview of where I come from, and my story in the Web business.

 

The Beginning

I was born in Quebec and hauled all over the East coast of Canada by my military family, and have become open minded about different cultures and social tendencies. Going back to early high school, I became very fond of making Web sites. Although I had a set path in engineering, I decided to turn to multimedia arts in my last year of high school, and applied in Graphic Design in 2005.

I graduated from La Cité collégiale in Graphic Design in 2008 and was accepted as a student member of the Registered Graphic Designers (RGD) of Ontario.

Getting Started

In order to touch as many aspects of the Web industry as possible, I began freelancing in college for Web design, where I was given small contracts by my teachers. In 2007, I was accepted by the Canadian Forces as a consultant to renew the Canadian Forces Support Unit of Ottawa's look and feel. This gave me a strong base with restrictions imposed on government sites, known as the Common Look and Feel 2 (CLF2).

After graduation, I began working as an Integrator for a small private company, where I learned quickly about the World Wide Web Consortium's (W3C) best practices, and with the aide of a good multimedia team I learned how to make full sites in little to no time.

Current Chapter

Today, I still work for the same private company, and have been transfered to a new office in Montreal. Since the begining, I have gained further experience with my new responsibilities in tech support, client trainings, project estimations, participating in sales pitches and leading teams in multimedia.

Make sure to check out my portfolio and my resumé in order to get the full scoop! You can also look for me on LinkedIn.
